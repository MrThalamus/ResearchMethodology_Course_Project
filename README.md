# Academic Procrastination among Undergraduate Students

## Overview

This research project investigates academic procrastination among undergraduate students and explores the relationship between academic procrastination and self-efficacy. The study focuses on identifying procrastination levels among AIUB undergraduate students and analyzing factors that influence delayed academic tasks. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Research Objectives

- Measure the level of academic procrastination among undergraduate students
- Analyze the relationship between academic procrastination and academic self-efficacy
- Identify factors influencing procrastination behavior
- Provide insights for improving academic performance and learning strategies

## Methodology

The study used a survey-based research approach. Data was collected from 121 undergraduate students through a structured Google Form questionnaire. The questionnaire included academic procrastination and self-efficacy scales. The collected data was analyzed using R programming with descriptive statistics and Pearson correlation analysis. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Key Findings

- Most students showed moderate to high levels of academic procrastination
- Living environment had noticeable influence on procrastination levels
- Gender differences in procrastination were minimal
- Academic self-efficacy showed a very weak and statistically insignificant relationship with procrastination (r = 0.06) :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Tools Used

- Google Forms (Data Collection)
- Microsoft Excel (Data Preparation)
- R Programming (Statistical Analysis)

## Research Scope

The study highlights the importance of structured academic support, better learning environments, time management strategies, and self-regulation skills to reduce academic procrastination among students. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
